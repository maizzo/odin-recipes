Pasta Genovese

What is Genovese?

Genovese sauce is a slow-cooked onion and meat sauce associated with Italy's Campania region, especially Naples — typically served with paccheri, ziti or candele pasta — and sprinkled with grated cheese. Genovese may be prepared with inexpensive cuts of beef, pork, veal or sausage, but typically share and emphasize slow-cooked onions. Recipes may cite the ramata di Montoro, a yellow onion with copper-colored skin.[1] Likely introduced to Naples from the northern Italian city of Genoa during the Renaissance, Genovese has since become associated with Italy's South, and especially Campania.

What ingredients do you need!

  • 320g Ziti or paccheri pasta
  • 2kl of yellow union
  • 75g Carrots
  • 1 bay leave
  • Extra Vergine olio
  • 600g gram Beef
  • 75g Celert
  • 1 sprig Parsley
  • 150g White Wine

Preparation

  1. Start peeling and thinly slicing the onions
  2. Peel and finely chop the Carrots
  3. Leave the tuft to one side and tie celery parsley laurel togheter
  4. Prepare meat by removing excess fat and cut in pieces
  5. Add generous amount olive oil in saucepan followed by onions
  6. Now put in the celery and carrots for a few minutes before adding meat
  7. add the bouqet garni and a pinch of salt, stir and leave for couple minutes
  8. stir once in a while agter 3 hours remove bouqet garni
  9. Add a part of the wine and simmer on high flame
  10. keep stiring for another hour
  11. gradually add the remaining wine as the sauce thickens
  12. Cook the pasta
  13. drain pasta and add to the sauce pan
  14. Stir well and add some pepper
  15. Plate the pasta en sptinkle with Parmigano
  16. Buon Appetito!!
Tips

A few tomatoes or a little tomato paste is added in some versions. Water is added in other versions, for even creamier onions. Some add lard, butter or pork, for an even richer genovese.
